Frequently Asked Questions

What is digital notarization?

Digital notarization, also known as electronic notarization or eNotarization, is the process of getting documents notarized electronically. It involves the use of electronic signatures and digital notary seals. The notary public verifies your identity remotely through secure video conferencing and validates your electronic signature on digital documents.

Is digital notarization legally binding?

Yes, digital notarization is legally binding in all 50 states under the Uniform Electronic Transactions Act (UETA) and the Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act (ESIGN). However, some specific document types or jurisdictions may have additional requirements. Our notaries are certified in accordance with all relevant state laws.

What documents can be notarized remotely?

Most common documents can be notarized remotely, including: affidavits, powers of attorney, loan documents, real estate deeds, wills, trusts, medical directives, and business agreements. However, some documents like birth and death certificates may require in-person notarization depending on state laws. Our system will notify you if your document requires in-person notarization.

How is my identity verified during remote notarization?

For remote notarization, we use a multi-factor identity verification process that includes: government-issued photo ID verification, knowledge-based authentication questions, and real-time video interaction with the notary. This process is more secure than traditional in-person notarization and complies with all relevant legal requirements.

How quickly can I get my document notarized?

With our remote online notarization service, you can often get your document notarized within 15-30 minutes of uploading it. For urgent needs, our priority service guarantees notarization within 1 hour during business hours. Mobile notary services require scheduling and typically need 24-48 hours advance notice, though same-day service may be available in some areas.

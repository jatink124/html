Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our telemedicine services

How does telemedicine work?

Telemedicine allows you to consult with healthcare providers remotely using video calls, phone calls, or secure messaging. Through our platform, you can discuss symptoms, receive diagnoses, get prescriptions, and receive follow-up careall without leaving your home.

Is telemedicine right for my condition?

Telemedicine is suitable for many conditions including colds, flu, allergies, rashes, UTIs, minor injuries, chronic disease management, mental health concerns, and follow-up care. However, medical emergencies like severe chest pain, difficulty breathing, or severe injuries require immediate in-person emergency care.

Are your doctors licensed?

Yes, all healthcare providers on our platform are licensed, board-certified professionals. You can view each doctor's credentials, specialties, and patient ratings before scheduling an appointment.

How do prescriptions work?

If your treatment requires medication, our doctors can send electronic prescriptions directly to your preferred pharmacy for pickup. Please note that regulations regarding certain controlled substances may limit what can be prescribed through telemedicine.

Does insurance cover telemedicine?

Many insurance plans now cover telemedicine services. We accept most major insurance providers. You can check coverage before your appointment by entering your insurance information during registration. For those without insurance coverage, our affordable self-pay options are clearly listed.
